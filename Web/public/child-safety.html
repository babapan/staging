Child Safety Standards

Last updated: May 27, 2026

🛡️

KyuLive has a zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). This page describes our safety standards, enforcement mechanisms, and how to report violations — in compliance with Google Play's Child Safety policy for social applications.

1. Our Commitment

KyuLive is a social platform for adults aged 18 and above. We are deeply committed to creating a safe environment and strictly prohibiting any content, behavior, or interaction that sexually exploits or endangers minors.

We comply with all applicable laws regarding child protection, including reporting obligations to relevant authorities.

2. Prohibited Content & Behavior

The following are strictly forbidden on KyuLive and will result in immediate account termination and reporting to law enforcement:

  • Any content depicting child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — including images, videos, audio, or text.
  • Grooming, soliciting, or communicating with minors for sexual purposes.
  • Sharing, distributing, or promoting CSAM in any form.
  • Using the platform to arrange or facilitate offline sexual contact with a minor.
  • Impersonating a minor to deceive other users.
  • Any sexualized depiction of minors, even if portrayed as fictional.

3. Age Verification & Access Controls

KyuLive requires users to provide their date of birth during registration. Accounts belonging to users under 18 are not permitted and will be permanently removed upon detection.

  • Date of birth is collected and verified at registration.
  • Users under 18 are blocked from completing registration.
  • Moderators and automated systems monitor for underage users and take immediate action.

4. Content Moderation

We use a combination of automated detection and human moderation to identify and remove CSAE content:

  • Automated scanning: All uploaded images and videos are scanned using hash-matching and AI-assisted detection to identify known CSAM.
  • Human review: Reports flagged by users or automation are reviewed by our Trust & Safety team within 24 hours.
  • Immediate removal: Confirmed CSAE content is removed immediately, the responsible account is terminated, and evidence is preserved for law enforcement.
  • Legal reporting: We report confirmed CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) via CyberTipline, and to applicable local authorities.

5. In-App Reporting

Every user, profile, message, post, and live stream has a built-in Report button. Reports related to child safety are escalated with the highest priority.

To report: tap the ⋯ menu on any profile, message, or stream → select Report → choose Child Safety → submit. Our team will respond within 24 hours.

6. Contact — Point of Contact (POC)

Our designated Child Safety Point of Contact is responsible for handling CSAE reports, coordinating with law enforcement, and ensuring policy compliance.

  • Email: safety@chatmeapp.my.id
  • Response time: Within 24 hours for child safety reports.

🚨 Report Child Safety Violation

If you have encountered content or behavior on KyuLive that you believe involves the sexual exploitation of a child, please contact us immediately:

  • In-app: tap ⋯ → Report → Child Safety
  • Email: safety@chatmeapp.my.id

You may also report directly to the authorities:

  • NCMEC CyberTipline (International)
  • Kementerian Kominfo (Indonesia)
Email Our Safety Team

7. Employee & Moderator Training

All Trust & Safety team members receive mandatory training on:

  • Identifying CSAM and grooming behavior patterns.
  • Proper handling and preservation of evidence.
  • Legal reporting obligations under applicable law.
  • Victim-centric practices to avoid re-traumatization.

8. Policy Updates

This Child Safety Standards document is reviewed and updated regularly. Any significant changes will be communicated through the app and on this page. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
